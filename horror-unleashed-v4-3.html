Section 01 — The Strategic Thesis

THE BRAND
VOICE PROBLEM

Brand strategy · audience targeting · the core insight
▾

Horror Unleashed has a strong identity and a live, open attraction. What it needs now is a marketing system built for the long game.

The brand voice works for fans.
It also needs to convert strangers.
What Exists

A Strong Horror Identity

The creepy POV voice, the dark aesthetic, the insider tone — it works beautifully for Universal loyalists and horror fans who already get it. That brand equity is real and worth protecting. We don't touch it. We build around it.

What's Missing

An Entry Point for Everyone Else

A first-time Vegas tourist scrolling TikTok doesn't have context for the insider voice. They see "unsettling brand account" and keep scrolling — instead of buying a ticket. The fix isn't changing the identity. It's adding a wider door into it.

The Brand's Own Icons

Jack & Chance Lead the Charge

Jack the Clown and Chance aren't borrowed IP — they're Horror Unleashed's own original characters, and they're already the heart of the attraction. Jack's Alley, Stay or Slay, the dark carnival energy — this is the brand's personality engine. The content strategy leans into them first and hardest.

The Accessibility Bridge

The Classic Monsters Open the Door

Dracula. The Mummy. Frankenstein. They're inside the Universal Monsters haunted house and they're universally recognizable — even to people who've never heard of Jack the Clown. We use the classic monsters as the handshake with new audiences: relatable, shareable entry points that lead people straight to the deeper brand. Jack and Chance are what they discover when they get inside.


Five Audiences. Five Distinct Strategies.
🎭

Horror Fans

The foundation this brand is built on. Deeper lore, character-voice content, exclusive access. Reward the faithful.

Repeat potential and word-of-mouth engines. They bring every visitor to town. Loyalty program, preview nights, micro-creator activation.

PR & Must-Do Lists

When someone Googles "things to do in Vegas" or watches a Vegas TikTok, Horror Unleashed should be in the first three options. That's a PR and creator strategy, not an ad buy.

Direct Mail

Section 03 — During the Visit

MAXIMIZE
THE MOMENT

UGC design · character moments · on-site activations
▾

Every group visit is a content creation event. Design the experience so the marketing happens automatically.

📱
UGC Engine

Design the Shareable Moment

Groups post when the experience gives them something worth posting. Identify the three highest-shareable moments and make them unmissable. The screamer, the tough guy pretending not to be scared, the one gripping someone's arm — that group dynamic IS the content. Seed a hashtag, feature the best posts, and every group visit markets the next one.

🎭
Jack & Chance

The Brand's Own Icons Are the Differentiator

Jack the Clown and Chance are Horror Unleashed's original characters — and they're already running the show in Jack's Alley. The Stay or Slay live performance is a content universe. A bachelorette group getting pulled into the act is a TikTok. Jack singling out the one person in the group who's trying to play it cool is a viral moment. Coordinate with ops to identify the highest-shareable character interactions and build content briefs around them. These moments don't happen by accident.

📧
Day-Of Email

The Pre-Visit Touchpoint

An email hits the morning of every booking. Character voice — Jack's voice, specifically — with practical visit info and a specific prompt to share their experience. "Jack knows you're coming tonight. He's been preparing. So has Chance." Sets the tone, builds anticipation, plants the share seed before they even arrive.

🤝
Promotions

In-Experience Partner Moments

In collaboration with the Global Promotions team: drink specials tied to character names, photo package upsells at peak engagement moments, co-branded merchandise that travels home. The visit doesn't end at the exit — it ends when the last person in the group posts about it.

Email — Post Visit

The Sequence That Retains

Post-visit email sequence in character voice. 24hr: "The monsters noticed you left. They have thoughts." 1-week: referral offer. 1-month: seasonal re-engagement. Locals segmented into a separate retention track.

  • 24hr post-visit: UGC prompt + review ask
  • 1-week: "Bring someone next time" referral
  • 1-month: new content teaser + return incentive
  • Locals → separate loyalty email track
UGC Amplification

Turn Visitors Into Marketing

Every tagged post is an impression we didn't pay for. Monitor, select, and amplify the best group UGC — featured on social, in email, in paid retargeting. Real group content converts better than any produced creative.

  • Weekly UGC review and amplification cadence
  • Best group content in paid social retargeting
  • UGC in lead nurture sequences for future bookings
  • Creator permission framework for top organic posts
Direct Mail — Retention

Physical Beats the Inbox

High-value group bookings get a physical follow-up — a thank you mailer, a next-visit offer, a group package teaser. In a world of emails, a physical touchpoint from a horror attraction goes on someone's desk, not their trash folder.

  • Post-visit mailer for groups of 10+
  • Seasonal campaign mailer to past visitors
  • Corporate group follow-up with event package info
  • Dark editorial design — collectible quality
Paid Social — Retargeting

Bring Them Back

Past visitors are the highest-converting audience. Retargeting built around seasonal activations and the full moon calendar gives past visitors a specific reason to return — and a specific date.

  • Seasonal activation retargeting: Feaster, Summertime Scaries, Creepmas
  • Full moon event retargeting — monthly touchpoint
  • "New since you visited" campaign cadence
  • Local Vegas audience repeat-visit incentive
Local Community

Vegas Residents Are the Foundation

Locals bring every visitor to town. A locals-specific program — discounted tickets, exclusive preview nights, early seasonal access — turns them into the most powerful word-of-mouth engine on the strip.

  • Vegas resident loyalty or membership program
  • Exclusive local preview nights for new content
  • Local micro-creator activation program
  • Community partnership with Vegas neighborhood groups
Lifecycle Reporting

Every Channel Reports to Revenue

Monthly lifecycle performance report to leadership. Email open rates to ticket attribution. UGC reach to conversion lift. Retargeting ROAS by segment. A coverage dashboard that connects media moments to business outcomes.

  • Monthly report to leadership — all channels
  • Email: open rate, CTR, ticket attribution
  • Paid social: ROAS by campaign and segment
  • UGC: reach, engagement, conversion lift

Section 06 — Vegas Event Crossovers

JACK CRASHES
EVERYTHING.

CES · UFC · F1 · EDC · conventions · planned & unplanned activations
▾

Vegas hosts some of the largest events in the world every single week. Most attractions ignore them. Horror Unleashed should be in the middle of all of them.

Planned Activations

Coordinated. Press-Ready. Timed to the Event.

For major events where advance coordination is possible — press is notified, photography is ready, characters are briefed with event-specific content. Horror Unleashed gets three beats of coverage: the tease, the moment, the reaction roundup. The story writes itself when you put Jack the Clown at a boxing weigh-in.

Unplanned Activations

No Announcement. Drop the Content After.

Characters deployed with a camera operator, no press release, no warning. The lack of announcement is the point — it feels real because it is. Someone at CES posts a video of Jack the Clown genuinely horrified by a humanoid robot and it gets half a million views before Horror Unleashed even reposts it. The best PR you didn't plan.

The Vegas Event Calendar — Horror Unleashed Edition
Unplanned January

CES — Jack vs. Technology

Jack wandering through CES genuinely bewildered by every product. Standing next to a humanoid robot doing a slow turn to camera. Chance perched on a self-driving car. "He attended CES. He had concerns." The contrast between analog horror chaos and sterile tech future is the bit — and it writes itself.

Unplanned January

SHOT Show — Chance on the Floor

The vibe at SHOT Show is already dark. Chance fits right in and still stops people cold. No announcement, just a camera operator and a character who belongs there more than anyone expected. The reactions are the content.

Planned March — April

March Madness — Group Night Push

Tens of thousands of sports fans in Vegas with a full entertainment budget and nothing planned after the game. Horror Unleashed as the late-night group activity. Targeted paid social to hotel guests during tournament weeks. Jack appears at watch parties. Sports media covers the crossover.

Planned April

NAB — Film Industry Homecoming

The broadcast and film industry comes to Vegas every April. Horror Unleashed is built by the same people who make the best haunted experiences in the world — film-quality sets, professional actors, practical effects. NAB is the room to tell that story. Press kit tailored to industry media, VIP industry night, trade coverage that reaches a completely different audience.

Unplanned May

EDC — Chance in the Crowd

EDC brings 170,000 people to Vegas over three days, skewing perfectly toward the Horror Unleashed audience. Chance could walk through the festival grounds and half the crowd wouldn't even blink. The other half posts it. Low effort. Massive reach. The costume fits right in — which somehow makes it more unsettling.

Planned June

UFC / Boxing Fight Week

Jack at the weigh-in. The chaos energy matches perfectly — and the international media coverage of fight week means the moment travels far beyond Vegas. Coordinate with the venue where possible. If not, public spaces outside the event center work just as well. A horror clown at a press conference is a press photo regardless of where he's standing.

Planned October

Comic-Con / Genre Conventions

Direct audience overlap — these people are already Horror Unleashed's customer. Full booth presence plus roaming characters. On-site ticket conversion, creator briefings, press access. The highest-ROI activation of the year because the audience is pre-sold. Show up, make it worth their while, convert on the spot.

Planned November

Formula 1 — International Reach

F1 Vegas brings a high-spend, internationally diverse crowd with enormous social media footprints. Horror Unleashed characters on the strip during race week. Co-branded content with hotel partners hosting F1 guests. The international coverage angle means a single activation reaches audiences in markets we'd never otherwise touch.

Planned Year Round

Convention Center — Corporate Groups

The Las Vegas Convention Center runs major trade shows and conventions almost every week of the year. Each one brings thousands of professionals who need a group dinner or team night out. Targeted direct mail to meeting planners timed to the convention calendar. A Horror Unleashed group package pitched to every corporate event coordinator in the city.

🃏

The through-line: Jack and Chance don't need permission to own Vegas. They just need to show up. Every activation — planned or not — generates content for Horror Unleashed's channels, coverage from the event's own media, and awareness from audiences who would never have found the attraction on their own. The events come to Vegas. We go to the events.
